How the "POC Brain" Pipeline Must OperateTo make this execution seamless for your engineering team, divide the POC Brain into three clean steps:The Cron Scraper: A daily or weekly Cron job crawls the target sitemaps. Each entry is tagged with its source type (e.g., source: textline_migration_faq).3. Executing the Indexing PullYou have two choices for moving data from your POC Brain's silo into Texitie's core app. Option A is the superior approach.Option A: The Automated Webhook Push (Recommended): The moment your POC Brain finishes processing and cleaning a batch of data, it fires a webhook to Texitie: POST /api/v1/library/update. Texitie receives the clean text and immediately indexes it into the local vector database. Why it wins: Data is always indexed and ready before a user ever sends a text message. Latency is non-existent during active chats.Option B: The Live Query Fetch (Dangerous): Texitie only searches the external POC Brain database when a customer asks a question it doesn't know. Why it fails: This reintroduces the exact latency loop you are trying to avoid, making your live text conversations feel sluggish.4.
