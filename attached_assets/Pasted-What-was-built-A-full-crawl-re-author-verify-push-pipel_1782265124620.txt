Thanks for sharing the vision — this is exactly the moment to pause, because there's a real tension between what you want and what this POC was deliberately built to do. Let me lay it out plainly.

Where you'd add URLs today
Right now there is no sitemap ingestion — the pipeline crawls a flat list of seed URLs you manage in Settings → seed URLs (stored in brain_config.seedUrls). You add full page URLs, hit Start New Run, and it crawls each one. There's no "give me a sitemap.xml and auto-discover every page" feature yet. That's buildable, but it doesn't exist.

The bigger issue: the build actively fights your vision
Here's the core thing. This POC's entire safety design is to strip out vendor/competitor-specific claims and keep only neutral, third-party industry facts. The two LLMs are explicitly instructed:

LLM #1 tags anything "brand-specific," "promotional," or about "a named company" as kind: "vendor_claim".
LLM #2 is a fail-closed verifier that rejects everything that isn't neutral industry knowledge.
The push gate only sends approved && kind === "industry". Vendor claims are never pushed — that's the headline invariant of the whole project.
So if you feed it your competitors' sites, the pipeline will do its job and throw away exactly the thing you're trying to capture. A competitor page saying "we offer 99.9% uptime, two-way MMS, and free number provisioning" is precisely what gets classified as a vendor_claim and blocked. What survives into your Library is the generic, non-competitive residue ("SMS supports 160 characters," "TCPA requires opt-in") — true, but not "build a knowledge base of competitor features."

In short: the current Brain is a competitor-claim filter, and you're asking it to be a competitor-claim harvester. Same plumbing, opposite policy.

How I'd reconcile it — pick a direction
There are a few honest paths, and they're genuinely different products:

Flip the policy to capture competitor features (your stated vision). Keep the crawl → re-author → verify architecture, but change the verifier's job from "reject anything brand-specific" to "extract competitor feature/pricing/capability facts, normalized into neutral capability statements." E.g. turn "AcmeSMS offers two-way MMS" into a library fact "Two-way MMS is an available SMS platform capability." This gives you a competitive feature library your other Replit can consume — without storing raw promotional spin.

Keep the safety filter but add a separate "competitive intel" track. Two libraries: the existing clean industry library, plus a new competitor-features store that keeps vendor_claims (attributed to the source) instead of blocking them. Nothing about the current invariant changes; you just stop discarding the vendor track.

Leave it as-is. Only makes sense if you actually want neutral industry knowledge and competitors are just convenient source text.

There's also a worth-knowing caveat regardless of direction: scraping competitor sites for feature data is a different legal/ToS posture than crawling Wikipedia. Many sites restrict it. Worth being deliberate about which competitors and pages.

My recommendation: Option 1 matches what you described most directly, and it's a moderate change (verifier prompt + kind/push policy + a category like competitor_feature), not a rebuild. Option 2 is more work but keeps both worlds.
