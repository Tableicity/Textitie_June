Tier 1: Categorical Metadata Scoping (The Fast Switch)When your Professor absorbs data, the backend must tag every piece of knowledge with a strict system category (e.g., pricing, compliance, technical_setup, features).Why it matters: When a customer texts "How much does an unregistered number cost?", your system should instantly restrict its search space to records tagged with the pricing category.The Benefit: Instead of your database searching through all 10 million tokens of tenant knowledge, it only searches a tiny pool of a few thousand tokens, dropping query latency from seconds to milliseconds.Tier 2: Hierarchical Chunk Indexing (The Precision Layer)Do not chunk your text into standard, uniform blocks (like 500 characters each). This loses vital context, causing your Student LLM to hallucinate or mix up details.The Parent-Child Approach: For highly technical sections like carrier regulations, break the documentation down into Child Chunks (small, highly specific 250-token blocks containing exact metrics) that are mapped to a larger Parent Chunk (the overall summary context of that policy).The Result: The database identifies the precise snippet containing the answer, but passes the overall conceptual context to your Student LLM, ensuring a highly accurate reply.2.     id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
